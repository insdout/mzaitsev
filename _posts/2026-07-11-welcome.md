Hi, and welcome to my blog. This is a placeholder first post while I get things
set up. Mostly, it exists to confirm that **LaTeX math** renders properly.

Inline math works like this: the Pythagorean theorem is $$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$$, and
Euler's identity, $$e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$$, is often called the most beautiful
equation in mathematics.

Display equations work too. Here is the Gaussian integral:

$$
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} \, dx = \sqrt{\pi}
$$

And the definition of the derivative:

$$
f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h}
$$

If the expressions above show up as typeset math rather than raw dollar-sign
text, everything is working. More real content coming soon.
